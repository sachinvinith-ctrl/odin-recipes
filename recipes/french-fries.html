French fries


Description

French fries, or simply fries, also known as chips, and finger chips, are batonnet or julienne-cut deep-fried potatoes of disputed origin. They are prepared by cutting potatoes into even strips, drying them, and frying them, usually in a deep fryer.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes: Russet or Idaho potatoes work best for their high starch/low water content.
  • Oil: Peanut, canola, or vegetable oil for deep frying.
  • Salt: Kosher salt or table salt for seasoning.
  • Optional Seasonings: Garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, chili powder, black pepper, oregano, or even truffle oil/Parmesan.

Steps

  1. Prep Potatoes: Wash, peel (optional), and cut potatoes into uniform 1/4-inch thick strips.
  2. Soak: Place cut potatoes in a bowl of cold water (with a little vinegar or salt) for at least 30 minutes to remove starch; rinse until water runs clear, then drain well.
  3. Dry: Pat potatoes completely dry with paper towels; this prevents oil splattering.

  5. Second Fry (Crisp): Increase oil temperature to 375-400째F (190-200째C). Fry the blanched potatoes for 1-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Season & Serve: Drain fries on paper towels and immediately season generously with salt and any other desired seasonings. Serve hot.
